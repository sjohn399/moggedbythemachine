I'm a bad software engineer.

Coding to me, has been like an arranged marriage.

It all started for practical reasons (see: $$$), but gradually over the years I started thinking she was kinda cute. She was more than javascript frameworks and Active Record queries. She was the magical representation of 1s and 0s into grand structures to solve grander problems. She compiled and rendered, she compressed and parsed, she assembled and zipped. She showed me what she was essentially and I was intrigued.

And then came the Machine. You know the one I'm talking about, strutting all over Twitter telling you that you should divorce your wife. Last year I told myself "this will all blow over" and I should just keep getting to know her. Then about a month ago I talked to the Machine and the Machine was pretty damn convincing. I can do anything you can do, but better. Now maybe for the S tier programmers out there this isn't true, but for the bad engineer it definitely is.

So do abandon my efforts to the Machine and let it do it all or delve into deep into the mysteries of software? What if in 5 years
