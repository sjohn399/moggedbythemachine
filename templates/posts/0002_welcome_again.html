I'm a bad software engineer twice.

Coding to me, has been like an arranged marriage.

It was a relationship born and nurtured by neccesity. She had the family fortune that would make up for my comically indolent and over-exciteable youth, I had just enough wit, charm, and potential to convince her parents that I was suitable if not choice. Money for value; as American as it gets. We've been bargaining and budgeting our relationship ever since.

But ya know, although they say familiarity breeds contempt; it also breeds plain, old familiarity. You see, I was a dutiful husband, not a bad one. I learned the Javascript frameworks that made her smile and the design patterns that satisfied. She would present me with problems and I would solve them. Sometimes I would work late if the problem interested me, sometimes I would shirk and cut corners if I thought she wouldn't notice and it didn't matter. It was never about devotion, only the tepid gray of tolerable neccesity.

Then, well I don't know, one day I looked up and thought "Damn, she's kinda cute." I don't know what changed about her or within me. She was the same Active Record query and I was the same Active Record querier. It must have been that slow accumulation of experience like the transfer of heat into a pot of water, and then in one moment quantity becomes quality and I'm boiling over trying to figure out what the C10K problem is and how epoll works.

But there's a problem I didn't expect - the Machine. You know the one. Mr. Steel Yo Girl. I'm told that with the Machine's arrival, I should file for divorce. Dario, Sam, and half of Twitter are all convinced of it. Maybe I can hang out in a room of Machines who I perpetuate my relationship with her through (the analogy is breaking down, what kind of pyramid polygamy horror is this?) But I don't want to, I just fell in love!

So I won't, I want to continue to type characters into Neovim and make my brain go "brrrrrrr". Because the "brrrrr" is the important part and when I use the Machine my brain stops "brrrrrrring". But to be clear - this is not a diatribe against the Machine. I think the Machine is very useful, although how useful is still yet to be seen. So I need some heuristics to guide my menage a trois between the Code, The Machine, y moi.

  1. Does it make me better or worse?
  2. Does it increase or decrease my indepdendence?
  3. Does it allow me to make things that are useful and beautiful?

If the answer to those things is negative, then I need to course correct. If the answer is positive, then I need to double down.

So to start, here's the most basic-bitch software project a man could do. A blog.

I'm a bad engineer. I want to be a good engineer. Hopefully that will continue to matter!
